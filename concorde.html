Concorde

A supersonic passenger aircraft designed for high-speed international travel.

Aircraft Overview

Concorde was a supersonic passenger aircraft developed by Britain and France. It could fly faster than twice the speed of sound and greatly reduce travel time across the Atlantic Ocean.

Specifications

Maximum Speed

Mach 2.04

Range

About 3,900 nautical miles

Engines

4 Rolls-Royce/Snecma Olympus 593 engines

First Flight

1969

Passengers

About 100

Primary Role

Supersonic passenger transport

Engineering Features

Delta Wing

Concorde used a delta-shaped wing designed for stable and efficient supersonic flight.

Droop Nose

The nose could move downward during takeoff and landing to improve pilot visibility.

Afterburning Turbojet Engines

Concorde used powerful engines with afterburners during takeoff and acceleration to supersonic speed.
