SR-71 Blackbird

A high-speed reconnaissance aircraft designed for extreme altitude and speed.

Aircraft Overview

The SR-71 Blackbird was developed by Lockheed's Skunk Works. Its speed, altitude, and specialized materials made it one of the most advanced aircraft of its era.

Specifications

Maximum Speed

Mach 3.3+

Maximum Altitude

Above 85,000 ft

Engines

2 Pratt & Whitney J58 engines

First Flight

1964

Crew

2

Primary Role

Strategic reconnaissance

Engineering Features

Titanium Structure

Much of the aircraft used titanium because ordinary materials could not handle the heat produced during sustained high-speed flight.

Special Engine Design

The J58 engines operated differently at high speeds and worked together with the aircraft's inlet system to maintain efficient flight.

Reduced Radar Signature

The aircraft used angled surfaces and specialized materials to reduce radar detection.
