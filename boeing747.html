Boeing 747

A wide-body passenger aircraft that transformed long-distance commercial aviation.

Aircraft Overview

The Boeing 747 became one of the most recognizable passenger aircraft in aviation history. Its large capacity and long range helped make international air travel more accessible.

Specifications

Cruising Speed

About Mach 0.85

Range

Up to about 8,000 nautical miles

Engines

4 turbofan engines

First Flight

1969

Passengers

Varies by model and configuration

Primary Role

Long-range passenger and cargo transport

Engineering Features

Wide-Body Design

The aircraft used a wide fuselage with two passenger aisles, allowing more passengers and cargo than earlier commercial aircraft.

Upper Deck

Its raised cockpit and partial upper deck created the aircraft's distinctive shape.

Long-Range Performance

Powerful turbofan engines and large fuel capacity allowed the Boeing 747 to operate on long international routes.
